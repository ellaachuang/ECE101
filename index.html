ABOUT

Yanik Jhaveri is a sophomore Electrical Engineering student at New York University, driven by a passion for innovation and problem-solving. As an honors student, Yanik plays a key role in the Special Projects division of NYU’s Concrete Canoe Engineering Team, where he focuses on AI-based paddling improvement. He is also a writer for Washington Square News, covering the latest university news. Recently, Yanik combined his engineering skills with practical design to build a fully functional automatic card shuffler and dealer. He is proficient at using various 3D-Printers, Metal, Vinyl, and Wood Laser Cutting Technologies, and soldering. With experience teaching younger students through his high school science club, he is committed to inspiring the next generation of engineers and making complex concepts accessible to all using hands-on class and homework.
